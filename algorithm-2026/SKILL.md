## How the Algorithm Works (2026)

The algorithm is a prediction engine: "Will this specific viewer enjoy this specific video right now?" It uses billions of data points across three signals.

### Signal 1: Engagement (Attention)
- **CTR**: Target 4%+ (10%+ is elite)
- **Average View Duration**: Aim for 50%+ retention
- **Session Time**: Do viewers continue watching AFTER your video?
- **Active Engagement**: Likes, comments, shares, saves, subscribes

### Signal 2: Satisfaction (THE 2025-2026 SHIFT)
**Satisfaction now outweighs raw watch time.** This is the biggest change.
- Post-watch survey responses
- Repeat viewing patterns
- "Not Interested" clicks (negative signal)
- Session continuation (viewers watch 2-3 more after yours = strong signal)

Implication: A 6-minute video with 80% retention BEATS a 20-minute video with 30% retention.

### Signal 3: Relevance (Topic Match)
- Title, description, tags, captions
- Spoken words + visual content (AI transcription analyzes your actual content)
- Channel history and niche consistency
- Audience type patterns

## 4-Layer Testing System

Videos are tested in expanding circles:
1. **Core Audience** — Subscribers and regulars (hours 1-6)
2. **Recent Viewers** — Past viewers who haven't subscribed (hours 6-24)
3. **Topic Matches** — People watching similar content (days 1-3)
4. **Adjacent Audiences** — Related-topic viewers (days 3-7, viral potential)

If a video fails at layer 1, it rarely reaches layer 4. First 24 hours are critical.

## Three Recommendation Surfaces

### Home Feed (Browse)
- Personalized, broad interests
- Strong packaging + first 5 seconds critical
- Feb 2026 change: shifted from broad categories to viewer watch history clusters
- Micro-niche creators gained advantage

### Suggested Videos (Watch Next)
- Related content during/after viewing
- Series consistency matters
- End screens drive this (always suggest specific next video)

### Search
- Relevance first, then satisfaction
- Small channels CAN outrank large ones with better query matching
- 35% of YouTube traffic still from search

## Major 2025-2026 Changes

- **Shorts fully decoupled** from long-form (late 2025). Experiment freely.
- **Channel-level evaluation**: Algorithm judges your channel as a whole, not just individual videos. Consistency matters.
- **New Creator Push**: Channels under 500 subs actively promoted. "YouTube Hype" endorsement feature for creators under 500K.
- **Shorts extended**: Max 3 minutes. Collaboration up to 5 co-authors.
- **Accelerated testing for small channels**: Strong early signals (CTR + retention) trigger broader testing in days, not weeks.
- **AI Content Labeling**: Mandatory in 2025. Channels that don't label AI content face reduced recommendations.

## Myths Busted

- "Daily posting beats weekly consistency" — FALSE. One quality/week > seven mediocre/day.
- "Tags drive growth" — FALSE. Thumbnails, titles, retention = 95% of discovery.
- "Longer videos always rank better" — FALSE. Match length to topic. Short + high retention wins.
- "Shorts hurt your long-form" — FALSE (since late 2025). Fully decoupled.

## Audit Checklist

When optimizing a channel or video:

- [ ] Is the video delivering satisfaction (not just watch time)?
- [ ] CTR above 4%? If not, title/thumbnail problem.
- [ ] Retention above 50% at midpoint? If not, content/editing problem.
- [ ] Does the viewer watch another video after yours? (Check session time)
- [ ] Is the channel consistent in niche? (Algorithm rewards consistency)
- [ ] Are Shorts being used for discovery and long-form for revenue?
- [ ] Is AI content labeled? (Penalty if not)
- [ ] Series format in use? (Gets algorithmic priority)
- [ ] End screens pointing to specific related video?
